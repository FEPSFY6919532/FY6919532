Abstract

     Social Engineering is becoming an increasingly greater threat to cyber security because attackers use people to get what they want rather than technical weaknesses. This paper explores how attackers use a variety of methods to manipulate users into giving them confidential information or clicking on malicious links. Common methods, such as phishing, smishing, and pretexting, are covered, along with the reasons how they exploit human vulnerabilities. These attacks can also have major implications on big organisations, through data breaches or financial loss. Finally, the paper talks about defensive measures that can be taken to prevent such attacks from happening. For example, spreading awareness on the matter, as well as multi-factor authentication. The overall aim of the paper is to highlight the threat of social engineering, but also to show that it is possible to prevent attacks if the right measures are in place.
